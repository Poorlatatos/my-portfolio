Project: Project Kowloon

In Project Kowloon you run around the Walled City of Kowloon, Hong Kong, in a 3D platformer. The game is inspired by the real-life Kowloon Walled City, a densely populated, ungoverned settlement in Hong Kong that was demolished in 1993. This project was created as part of a game jam, and it was a great opportunity to learn about level design, character animation, and game mechanics.

Look around and see the spine-chilling thrill of being stuck in an enclosed city.

Key Features

  • **Exploration**: Traverse the narrow alleyways and rooftops of Kowloon Walled City.
  • **Platforming**: Jump, climb, and swing your way through the city's unique architecture.
  • **Collectibles**: Discover hidden items and secrets scattered throughout the city.

Evade through all sorts of obstacles in your path.

Developer's Reflection

Making Project Kowloon was a challenging yet rewarding experience. I learned a lot about game design, level creation, and character animation. The project allowed me to experiment with different mechanics and art styles, and I'm semi-proud of the final result. The problem was I didn't have enough time to polish the game, but I'm happy with what I was able to achieve in the given timeframe. Especially given that I was relatively new to game development at the time.
